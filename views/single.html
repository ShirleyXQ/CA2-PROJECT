Beijing is the world’s largest city with seven (7) world heritage sites and is the world’s first capital city with a global geopark. Beijing has more than 200 tourist attractions open to the outside world, including the world’s largest imperial palace, the Forbidden City, the Temple of Heaven, the Royal Garden Beihai Park, the Summer Palace, and the Yuanmingyuan Garden. The Badaling Great Wall, the Mutianyu Great Wall, and the world’s largest siheyuan The palace and other places of interest. Beijing has a total of 7,309 cultural relics and historic sites, 99 national key cultural relics protection units (including Beijing section of the Great Wall and Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal), 326 municipal cultural relics protection units, 5 national geological parks, and 15 national forest parks.

Custom products

Beijing is one of the four ancient capitals of China. There are many local customs: Beijing snacks, Peking opera, Beijing rhyme drums, comic dialogues, stage dramas, iron plates, cloisonne, tooth carvings, monkeys, lacquer carvings, celluloids and baskets, sugar candies, and pasta noodles. Wait.

Shopping district

Beijing is the only Mainland city to be selected as one of the top 15 shopping centers in the world and has more than 100 large and medium-sized shopping malls. Wangfujing Street, Qianmen Dashilan and Xidan Commercial Street are Beijing's traditional business districts; China World Shopping Mall, Oriental Xintiandi and Zhongguancun Plaza. The Old City of Liulichang or Panjiayuan has a variety of antiques. Xiushui Street and Yaxiu apparel wholesale market sell all kinds of clothing. Cloisonne, jade, and silk embroidery have a long history; folk arts and crafts, such as clay figurines, Beijing opera masks, kites, and paper-cuts, can be used as gifts for relatives and friends.
